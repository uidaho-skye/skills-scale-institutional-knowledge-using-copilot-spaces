# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria, discuss technical feasibility
- **Project Managers**: Provide effort estimates, report progress and blockers
- **Business Analysts**: Seek clarification on business requirements and use cases
- **QA Lead**: Collaborate on test strategies, coordinate defect fixes, ensure testability
- **Release Coordinator**: Support deployment activities, provide technical runbooks

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Align on priorities, timelines, and resource allocation
- **Developers**: Provide feature context, review designs, approve trade-offs
- **Business Analysts**: Collaborate on requirements definition, validate business cases
- **QA Lead**: Define quality expectations and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders**: Gather feedback, communicate product vision and roadmap updates

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Align on scope, priorities, and success metrics
- **Developers**: Track progress, remove blockers, coordinate sprint planning
- **Business Analysts**: Incorporate requirements into project plans, validate scope
- **QA Lead**: Monitor quality risks, coordinate testing timelines
- **Change Manager**: Assess change impact on project schedule and scope
- **Release Coordinator**: Synchronize release planning with project milestones

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholder needs and technical requirements. They document use cases, analyze business processes, and ensure requirements are well understood by both business and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements and use cases
- Translate business needs into functional specifications
- Conduct gap analysis and process mapping
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Maintain traceability between requirements and deliverables

### Goals
- Ensure alignment between business needs and technical solutions
- Reduce rework through clear, complete requirements
- Improve communication between business and technical teams
- Support data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Product Owner and developers
- Business case presentations and analysis reports
- User story refinement and acceptance criteria definition

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on defining features and priorities, validate business value
- **Project Managers**: Provide requirements updates, support planning and estimation
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements, answer questions, validate technical approaches
- **QA Lead**: Define acceptance criteria, support test case development

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads develop and execute testing strategies to ensure quality standards are met throughout the development lifecycle. They drive defect resolution, advocate for quality practices, and maintain test infrastructure.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and quality standards
- Plan and coordinate testing activities across sprints
- Develop and maintain automated test suites
- Track and prioritize defects with development team
- Conduct test case reviews and ensure coverage
- Report quality metrics and testing status
- Advocate for quality best practices and process improvements

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high test coverage and automation
- Reduce cycle time for quality validation
- Build quality into the development process

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning participation
- Test plan reviews and defect triage sessions
- Quality metrics reports to stakeholders
- Collaboration with developers on testability

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Review code for testability, coordinate defect fixes, pair on test automation
- **Project Managers**: Report testing status, flag quality risks, support release decisions
- **Business Analysts**: Validate acceptance criteria, ensure test coverage aligns with requirements
- **Release Coordinator**: Confirm release readiness, provide sign-off on quality gates

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee change requests, assess their impact on projects and systems, and ensure smooth transitions. They communicate changes to stakeholders and coordinate approval processes.

### Responsibilities
- Evaluate and prioritize incoming change requests
- Assess impact on project scope, timeline, and resources
- Coordinate Change Advisory Board (CAB) meetings when needed
- Communicate change decisions and implications to stakeholders
- Track change implementation and post-change validation
- Maintain change logs and audit trails
- Ensure change management policy compliance

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from changes to ongoing projects
- Maintain transparency in change decision-making
- Balance agility with controlled, predictable changes
- Reduce change-related incidents and rollbacks

### Typical Communication
- Change request reviews and impact assessments
- CAB meetings and approval communications
- Stakeholder notifications of approved/rejected changes
- Post-implementation reviews

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate change impact on project plans, communicate schedule/scope adjustments
- **Developers**: Understand technical implications, coordinate implementation timing
- **Business Analysts**: Assess business impact, validate requirements changes
- **Release Coordinator**: Align changes with release schedules, ensure proper deployment planning

---

## Release Coordinator

### Role Summary
Release Coordinators plan and orchestrate releases across teams and environments. They align schedules, coordinate deployment activities, and ensure all release prerequisites are met.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release schedules and calendars
- Coordinate deployment activities across multiple teams
- Verify release readiness and gate criteria
- Manage release communication and stakeholder notifications
- Track deployment progress and post-release verification
- Coordinate rollback procedures if needed
- Document release processes and lessons learned

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Improve release cycle time and reliability
- Ensure cross-team coordination and visibility

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings and readiness reviews
- Deployment status updates and go/no-go decisions
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Post-release retrospectives

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Align release timing with project milestones, communicate dependencies
- **QA Lead**: Verify test completion and quality gates, coordinate UAT and production validation
- **Developers**: Coordinate code freeze, deployment procedures, and environment readiness
- **Change Manager**: Ensure changes are approved and aligned with release windows
